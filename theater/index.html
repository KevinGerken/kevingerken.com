About Us

Charles Jackson IV willed his entire, considerable, fortune to his grandson Charles Jackson VI, provided he could utterly waste one million dollars. He chose to reopen the historic Smith Theater. It seemed like a sure fired way to go broke, but he underestimated the boredom level in a small town. Month to month despite his best efforts he made a slight profit. After a year the terms of the will became null and void. He is now trapped in a prison of his own accidental design.
